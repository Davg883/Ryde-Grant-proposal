Bypassing the NEET "Catch-22"

Strategic Delivery Priorities

Our programme directly executes the Isle of Wight Foundation's dual priorities by bridging cutting-edge technology and local heritage to solve real human problems.

💼

Priority 1: Access to Employment & Skills

Upskilling 30 Ryde NEET youth in advanced, future-proof digital engineering—including local-first system operations, TypeDB semantic database design, and 3D additive manufacturing. Graduates transition to paid local support roles within our independent hospitality network.

🌺

Priority 2: Building Communities & Combating Isolation

Youth apprentices design and print physical local-history postcards. In partnership with Operation Geranium, cards are hand-delivered alongside potted geraniums to isolated seniors, linking them directly to our virtual "Visit Ryde" trail.

🛡️

Innovation: Offline-First Sovereign Technology

By owning our high-performance GPU workstations and utilising TypeDB as a local truth ledger, we eliminate expensive cloud subscriptions, guarantee complete data privacy (GDPR compliance), and ensure absolute immunity to AI hallucinations. For our audio voiceovers, we bypass physical recording hardware by utilising Gemini 3.5 Flash's text-to-speech engine to render authentic Received Pronunciation (RP) voiceovers.

Courseware & Core Skills

The VibeAI Training Curriculum

Our 12-week programme utilises structured, entry-level curriculum books to train students in the core AI and digital engineering skills required to bypass the NEET Catch-22.

AI for Text

Teaches the fundamentals of semantic data prompting, digital copywriting, and structural data curation using Google AI Studio.

AI for Media

Focuses on content compilation, digital asset manipulation, and offline video compilation using standard tools.

AI for Code

Introduces programmatic logic, local-first code deployment, JSON manifests, and video compilation scripting in Remotion.

🛡️

Google Ecosystem & Personal Cloud Environments

To ensure absolute security, simplicity, and ease of onboarding in the first instance, the programme integrates directly with the **Google Ecosystem**. Using standard Google accounts, students can access easy, secure logins and manage their own cloud workspace. Data and work remain in the user's personal environment, eliminating complex IT administration and security overhead.

12-Month Project Delivery

The Implementation Roadmap

Months 1–2 (Jan - Feb 2026)

Albion Frequency AI Design Centre Setup

Physical setup of the AI Design Centre inside Department Ryde's Fitting Room. Installation of two high-performance GPU workstations (NVIDIA RTX) for local rendering, one outward-facing 4K display storefront screen, and one industrial-grade 3D printer for producing weatherproof waypoint mounts.

Months 3–11 (Mar - Nov 2026)

Three 12-Week Youth Cohorts

Training 30 local young adults in partnership with Network Ryde. Cohorts progress from digital copywriting and Vibe AI prompting (Weeks 1-4) to data archiving and video compilation (Weeks 5-8), and deploying menus & local-first network nodes (Weeks 9-12).

Months 10–12 (Oct - Dec 2026)

High Street Deployment & Integration

Apprentices deploy the modular "Sovereign Can" physical beacons and QR codes along Ryde Esplanade. Configured digital systems go live inside independent business venues, and the digital curriculum is integrated into Shademakers' National Saturday Club.
