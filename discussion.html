1. Executive Summary

This briefing outlines the contractual and operational terms of the collaborative bid submitted to the **Isle of Wight Foundation** for the **Monday, 15 June 2026** deadline. The total grant requested is **£14,000**, reserved entirely for material capital and equipment expenditure.

The programme operates on the **Sovereign Partnership Model**, dividing administrative responsibility, spatial usage, and technology implementation between Shademakers UK and Vectis ONE. By utilising Department Ryde's Fitting Room as the physical workspace and drawing on the proven operational success of the Bembridge Fort Open Day, the bid presents an audited, low-risk execution plan designed for maximum trust.

2. Division of Responsibilities

To meet strict grant guidelines, the division of labor represents a complete segregation of fiduciary oversight and technical delivery:

  • Shademakers UK (The Fiduciary Lead): Acts as the lead applicant and contracting authority. Shademakers holds overall legal and financial responsibility under the Isle of Wight Foundation SLA. They manage drawdowns, financial reporting, and the procurement trail.
  • Isle Connect / Vectis ONE (The Delivery Contractor): Operates as the technical, training, and design contractor. Vectis ONE delivers the training curriculum, configures the offline database systems, installs the hardware, and oversees the local high street deployment.

💡 Strategic Positioning

Structuring the application with Shademakers UK as the fiduciary applicant bypasses the standard technical agency "startup risk." Shademakers brings an established £2.4 million funding track record and pre-existing institutional trust with the Isle of Wight Council (IWC) and Ryde Neighbourhood Board (RNB).

🎙️ Audio Infrastructure Rationalisation

Bypassing physical recording equipment is factually justified: our live Vercel demonstration proved that the Gemini 3.5 Flash Text-to-Speech (TTS) engine (configured to the non-rhotic "Algieba" style) delivers authentic, high-fidelity Received Pronunciation (RP) voiceovers at zero hardware cost. By redirecting those funds into two high-performance GPU workstations, a large 4K storefront display, and an industrial 3D printer, we transform the Fitting Room into an advanced "additive manufacturing and AI design centre." The 3D printer enables youth apprentices to physically print custom, weather-proof PLA mounts to secure the "Sovereign Can" waypoints to historic brickwork without damaging protected Grade II facades.

5. Intellectual Property & Code Ownership

This section establishes clear boundaries regarding software intellectual property (IP), proprietary assets, and open-source contributions:

  • Proprietary Core: The software base—including the *Sovereign Heritage OS*, the *Wander Protocol*, and the *VibeAI* curriculum formulas—remains the sole intellectual property of Vectis ONE. Shademakers UK is granted a perpetual, non-exclusive, royalty-free licence to run the local system within their premises and operations.
  • Historical Data & Archive Assets: The verified historical data, images, and audio narration records compiled by Tim Wander (CEng) remain under the sovereign stewardship of the community and local curators.
  • Physical Capital: As required by the grant conditions, all physical hardware purchased using the £14,000 Isle of Wight Foundation grant remains the permanent, unencumbered property of Shademakers UK.

6. Risk Management & Safeguarding

The programme operates under strict statutory protocols to protect young adult apprentices and isolated senior citizens.

  • Youth Cohort Safeguarding: Delivered in strict alignment with Network Ryde’s established, pre-drafted organisational policies. All personnel involved in direct training delivery must hold active Enhanced DBS checks.
  • Grassroots Delivery Integrity: Postcards and physical geraniums will be distributed in partnership with Operation Geranium (Steve Double) and Age UK Isle of Wight, utilising experienced local workers who maintain existing, trusted relationships with isolated households.
  • Data Privacy & GDPR: The system is designed local-first. All data compiled from users scanning the "Sovereign Cans" or navigating the trail is held on the local GPU nodes in Ryde. No user tracking or behavioural data is sent to external, third-party cloud servers.
  • Cloud Ecosystem Integration: In the first instance, the training delivery utilises the standard Google Ecosystem. By leveraging Google accounts and Google Workspace, students log in easily and manage their data and files within their own personal cloud environments. This ensures data sovereignty, mitigates complex security administration, and reduces operational overhead in the setup phase.

The project establishes 'The Albion Frequency AI Design Centre' inside Department Ryde’s Fitting Room to execute the Foundation's dual priorities of Access to Employment and Building Better Communities [11, 15].

Access to Employment: We are addressing high youth unemployment by training Ryde’s vulnerable youth (aged 16–25) in advanced, future-proof digital engineering—including local-first system operations, semantic database design (TypeDB), and 3D additive manufacturing [11, 18, Vibe Coding]. Building Better Communities: We are bringing Ryde’s unique history to life [11]. Our apprentices will use their new skills to design and print physical, local-history postcards [11]. In partnership with Operation Geranium and Age UK Isle of Wight, these postcards will be hand-delivered alongside fresh, locally grown potted geraniums to isolated seniors across the Island, combatting deep-seated loneliness and social exclusion [1.3.2].

  • HPC Workstations: Two high-performance GPU workstations (NVIDIA RTX) for local, offline AI rendering and dataset training [11].
